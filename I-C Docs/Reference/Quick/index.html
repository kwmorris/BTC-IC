The homework (i.e. worksheet questions) for each of these courses include reading assignments, many of which are based on specific industry documents such as accident reports, instrument datasheets, and product manuals. All of these industry documents assigned to you for reading in the homework are contained in this Instrumentation Reference, and organized per course. This saves you the time and trouble of having to search for these documents on the internet, and also ensures everyone references the same version of each document.

INST 241 -- Temperature and Flow Measurement

  • Tables and Diagrams of the Thermal Properties of Saturated and Superheated Steam by Lionel Marks and Harvey Davis, published in 1920 by Longmans, Green, and Company. This is an easy-to-reference set of saturated and superheated steam tables, all expressed in British units (e.g. BTU, lb, degrees F) in 1 PSIA and 1 degree F increments. Pressures expressed in ``pounds'' are all PSIA (pounds per square inch absolute). The ``Total Heat of Steam'' (H or h) is otherwise known as enthalpy, expressed in BTU per pound mass. The superheated steam tables show the properties at the saturation temperature, then for 10 degree F increments of ``superheat.''
  • Thermocouple and RTD tables, by the Pyromation corporation.
  • Three Mile Island -- a report to the commissioners and to the public, courtesy of the Nuclear Regulatory Commission. This report presents a step-by-step accounting of the incident in easy-to-read prose.
  • Improving Thermocouple Service Life in Slagging Gasifiers, by James Bennett of the Department of Energy Albany Research Center (DOE/ARC). A report of some of the technical difficulties encountered with temperature measurement inside coal and biomass gasifier process units, which use extremely high temperatures to break organic matter down into more useful fuel gases.
  • Single-Crystal Sapphire Optical Fiber Instrumentation for Coal Gasifiers, DOE Sensors & Controls report. This short document describes the technology of Fabry-Perot interferometry applied to sapphire crystals, used to measure high temperatures in chemically corrosive environments.
  • Moore Industries Site-Programmable Temperature transmitter (SPT)
  • Rosemount high-temperature thermocouple assemblies
  • Moore (Siemens) model 33 pneumatic temperature transmitter
  • Investigation Report -- chemical manufacturing incident, courtesy of the US Chemical Safety Board. This report followed the 1998 Morton International, Inc. dye manufacturing accident (runaway chemical reaction) in Paterson, New Jersey. A critical temperature limit on yellow dye was exceeded, causing the normally exothermic reaction to ``run away'' beyond the cooling system's ability to remove heat.
  • Safety Bulletin -- Management of Change, courtesy of the US Chemical Safety Board. This safety bulletin describes the need for ``Management of Change'' procedures to help guide personnel through situations where conditions are significantly different from historical ``normals''. Two case studies are cited as examples: one at the Equilon refinery in Anacortes, Washington (coker fire) and another at a CONDEA Vista detergent processing facilty in Baltimore, Maryland.
