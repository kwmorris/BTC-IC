Master instrument loop number list

  • Loop system number assignments, a plain-text file listing all the loop numbers, IP network addresses, and other assigned data for permanently-installed instruments in the BTC Instrumentation lab as well as off-campus sites. Loop numbers and network addresses have been chosen such that there cannot be any conflicts, even if the instruments in question were brought together in the same location.

BTC Fisheries hatchery facilities

Bellingham Technical College's Fisheries Technology program operates two hatcheries: one for salmon and one for trout. The salmon hatchery is called the Perry Center, and is located on ``C'' street near Holly street in old-town Bellingham. The trout hatchery is located at Whatcom Falls Park along Lakeway Drive.

  • Generic loop diagram for Perry Center wet lab PLCs, a PDF document showing a generic loop diagram for Allen-Bradley MicroLogix 1100 PLCs located at the ends of each egg tray row, for measuring water flow, temperature, etc.
  • Ethernet network diagram Perry Center building, a PDF scan of a hand-written document showing the structured Cat-5 cabling used for all control system Ethernet connections in the Perry Center building.

Cedar Grove Park

A retirement community named Cedar Grove Park located on Samish Way (near Lake Padden) in Bellingham has invited BTC Instrumentation students to construct projects for their use. Our first project there was a water flow monitor for their potable water distribution piping system, which used to suffer regular and costly leaks. Our second project there was a water quality monitoring system for the small lake in the center of their community.

  • Photograph of the RTU sensing incoming water flow from the City of Bellingham, this RTU consists of a Koyo ``CLICK'' PLC sensing a contact signal from a water flowmeter (1 pulse per gallon) and relaying that data through a 900 MHz radio link to an HMI unit installed at the maintenance shop.
