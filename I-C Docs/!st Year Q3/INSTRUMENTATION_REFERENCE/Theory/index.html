General resources (textbooks, etc.)

  • Lessons in Industrial Instrumentation, a general Instrumentation textbook by Tony R. Kuphaldt. Published under the terms and conditions of the Creative Commons Attribution License, allowing free and open use, copying, distribution, editing, and re-publishing. The book file does not reside within this reference collection, but must be downloaded using an internet connection.
  • Example control loop wiring diagram, by Tony Kuphaldt. This diagram shows how a simple control loop may be constructed around a Honeywell model UDC2000 controller.
  • Using the model 13A D/P cell to measure flow and level (material provided courtesy of Invensys Process Systems, Foxboro, Massachusetts). Although this text is specific to one model of differential pressure transmitter, the techniques of flow and level measurement apply to all DP transmitters.

Measurement (sensors, transmitters, etc.)

  • Temperature
  • Improving Thermocouple Service Life in Slagging Gasifiers, by James Bennett of the Department of Energy Albany Research Center (DOE/ARC). A report of some of the technical difficulties encountered with temperature measurement inside coal and biomass gasifier process units, which use extremely high temperatures to break organic matter down into more useful fuel gases.
  • Single-Crystal Sapphire Optical Fiber Instrumentation for Coal Gasifiers, DOE Sensors & Controls report. This short document describes the technology of Fabry-Perot interferometry applied to sapphire crystals, used to measure high temperatures in chemically corrosive environments.

  • Pressure
  • Pressure indicating gauge types, by the Wika corporation. A brief, nontechnical introduction to pressure gauges.
  • Detection of Plugged Impulse Lines using Statistical Process Monitoring Technology, by the Rosemount corporation. An explanation of plugged-line detection as implemented in the 3051S series of Rosemount pressure transmitters.

  • Flow
  • Fundamentals of Orifice Meter Measurement, by the Daniel corporation.
  • Flow measurement manual, by the Emerson corporation. Covers AGA (American Gas Association) report 7, report 3, and report 8 flow measurement equations.
  • Electrical conductivity of process liquids A reference for Foxboro magnetic flow transmitters, this reference is generally useful for any brand of magflow instrument (document provided courtesy of Invensys Process Systems, Foxboro, Massachusetts).
  • Top Mount Installation for DP Flowmeters in Steam Service, a whitepaper written in 2009 discussing the benefits of mounting Annubar and compact orifice flow-measurement elements above steam lines rather than below, to avoid wet-leg problems.

  • Analytical
  • Electrodeless conductivity, toroidal magnets, and the procedure of degaussing, material reproduced courtesy of Invensys Process Systems, Foxboro, Massachusetts. An overview of how toroidal (inductive) liquid conductivity probes work.
  • BTU Analysis Using a Gas Chromatograph, by the Emerson corporation.
  • Fundamentals of Gas Chromatography, by the Emerson corporation.
  • Analysis of Pipeline-Quality Gas using a C9 Application (gas chromatography), by the Emerson corporation.

Control Valves and other Final Control Elements

  • Control Valves
  • Control Valve Handbook, by the Fisher corporation. An excellent handbook on control valve theory and selection.
  • Fundamentals of Gas Pressure Regulation (technical monograph 27), by Floyd D. Jury (Fisher corporation). A technical discussion of automatic (self-contained) gas pressure regulating valves.

Systems (data acquisition, control, networks, and documentation)

  • Symbols and Diagrams
  • ISA flowsheet symbol library for AutoCAD, by Tony Kuphaldt. A library of flowsheet (P and ID as well as loop diagram) symbols, complete with tag name attributes, for AutoCAD.
  • Loop Diagram Examples, by Tony Kuphaldt. This collection of loop diagrams shows some of the systems my students and I built during the 2002-2003 academic year. Note that the "US" and "UIR" instrument symbols represent a selector switch and general-purpose chart recorder (respectively), which we used in a very small control panel to monitor several process signals, one at a time.
  • Ladder diagrams for documenting PLC circuits, by Tony Kuphaldt. A short tutorial with template pages for creating your own easy-to-read wiring diagrams for PLC-controlled systems.
  • FOUNDATION Fieldbus networks and instruments
  • Foundation Fieldbus Blocks, by the Rosemount corporation. This document not only provides comprehensive descriptions of various FOUNDATION Fieldbus function blocks, but it also provides an excellent overview of Fieldbus in general in the first chapter. A must-read for anyone wanting to know how Fieldbus control works.
  • Fieldbus wiring guidelines A guide to proper wiring techniques for 31.25 kbps digital instrument networks, including termination resistor location, shielding, and other important details (material provided courtesy of Invensys Process Systems, Foxboro, Massachusetts).

Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS)

  • Process Automation Engineer's Guide, by the Pepperl+Fuchs corporation. A detailed yet very readable guide to safety issues in instrumentation: classified areas, intrinsic safety, SIL levels, etc.
  • Fisher FIELDVUE model DVC6000 series digital positioners for safety instrumented systems (SIS). While this is a product bulletin, it also contains basic information on partial-stroke safety valve testing.

Reference tables

  • General
  • Tables and Diagrams of the Thermal Properties of Saturated and Superheated Steam by Lionel Marks and Harvey Davis, published in 1920 by Longmans, Green, and Company. This is an easy-to-reference set of saturated and superheated steam tables, all expressed in British units (e.g. BTU, lb, degrees F) in 1 PSIA and 1 degree F increments. Pressures expressed in ``pounds'' are all PSIA (pounds per square inch absolute). The ``Total Heat of Steam'' (H or h) is otherwise known as enthalpy, expressed in BTU per pound mass. The superheated steam tables show the properties at the saturation temperature, then for 10 degree F increments of ``superheat.''
  • Fill Fluid Specifications, by the Rosemount corporation. This reference specifically cites fill fluids available for Rosemount's model 1199 pressure transmitter, but the information is generally useful for a variety of instruments.
  • Thermocouple and RTD tables
  • Thermocouple and RTD tables, by the Pyromation corporation.
